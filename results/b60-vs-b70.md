# Arc Pro B60 vs B70 — single-card head-to-head (TRX50)

The returned-from-RMA Sparkle **B60 (G21, 24 GB)** dropped into the TRX50 alongside a
**B70 (G31, 32 GB)**, same models, single card each. (Firmware audit: still on the "brick"
FWCODE 21.1177 + OptionROM 23.1065.0.0 and fully working → never bricked, RMA dropped, card kept.)

| Model | backend | B60 pp512 | B60 tg128 | B60 W | B70 pp512 | B70 tg128 | B70 W |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Qwen3-4B-Q4 | SYCL | 2236 | **101.8** | 81 | 3280 | 115.7 | 144 |
| Qwen3-4B-Q4 | Vulkan | 1698 | 55.4 | 72 | 2390 | 79.9 | 138 |
| Qwen3.6-35B-A3B-Q4_0 | Vulkan | 908 | 37.3 | 60 | 1250 | 48.0 | 96 |
| Qwen3.6-35B-A3B-Q4_0 | SYCL | — | — | — | 980 | 71.8 | 77 |

**Takeaways:**
- **Generation: B60 ≈ 78–88 % of a B70** (88 % on the SYCL production path).
- **Prompt processing: B60 ≈ 68–73 %** — the B70's extra Xe cores show on compute-bound prefill.
- **Power: B60 draws ~56–63 % of a B70** (60–81 W vs 96–144 W) → **1.2–1.6× more tokens per Joule**.
- **VRAM: 24 GB (21.5 usable) vs 32 GB** — B60 fits a 20 GB MoE fine but has 8 GB less headroom.
- **B60 POSTs** (G21 has mature DP+HDMI GOP) — it replaced the 750 Ti as the TRX50 console GPU,
  so its slot doubles as console **and** compute (the B70 G31 produces no POST video).

**Verdict:** the B60 is the **efficiency + console** card; the B70 is the **VRAM + prompt-throughput**
card. Keep the B60 as a standalone/console device — do **not** put it in a tensor-split (`-sm layer`)
with B70s (it bottlenecks them and the 24/32 GB mismatch wastes the B70's extra VRAM). For the
TRX50's big-model mission, VRAM is the bottleneck → expansion should be **uniform B70s**, not B60s;
the B60's efficiency edge is better exploited on an always-on box than on the idle-sleeping TRX50.
